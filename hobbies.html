My Hobbies

Exploring Nature, One Trail at a Time

My favorite hobbies revolve around hiking and being immersed in nature. There's something incredibly peaceful and rejuvenating about exploring trails, breathing in fresh mountain air, and witnessing breathtaking views from high elevations. Whether it's the challenge of a steep ascent or the serenity of walking through a forest, hiking allows me to disconnect from the busy world and reconnect with myself and the natural environment around me.

Some of my most memorable hiking experiences include climbing Mt. Josephine on the border of Canada, where the rugged terrain and stunning vistas made every step worthwhile. The sense of accomplishment upon reaching the summit, combined with the panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness, is something I'll never forget. Another unforgettable adventure was climbing Mt. Pilchuck in Washington, where the trail led through beautiful alpine meadows and rocky outcrops before revealing spectacular views from the historic fire lookout at the top.

Why This Matters to Me

Hiking and spending time in nature are more than just hobbies to me—they're essential to my well-being and personal growth. In our fast-paced, technology-driven world, these outdoor adventures provide a crucial balance to my academic life and future career in accounting. When I'm on a trail, surrounded by towering trees or standing at a mountain summit, I find clarity and perspective that's impossible to achieve anywhere else.

These experiences have taught me valuable lessons about perseverance, patience, and the importance of taking things one step at a time—qualities that translate directly to my studies and professional goals. The physical challenge of hiking keeps me healthy and energized, while the mental peace I find in nature helps me manage stress and maintain focus. Most importantly, these adventures remind me that there's more to life than spreadsheets and deadlines. They keep me grounded, humble, and grateful for the natural beauty that surrounds us.
